# 12 Bible Stories About Faith for Kids (Trust God Like Abraham)

Looking for Bible stories that teach your child about faith? These 12 stories are the best examples of faith in the Bible, retold simply for kids ages 5+.

## Why Teach Kids About Faith Through Bible Stories?

Children learn values through stories, not lectures. When your child watches David face Goliath or Ruth stay loyal to Naomi, they are not just hearing about faith. They are seeing it in action. These stories give kids a vocabulary for faith that they carry into their own lives.

---

### 1. Abraham Leaves Home
God says go, Abraham goes, destination unknown.

### 2. Noah Builds the Ark
Building a boat when there is no rain.

### 3. Moses at the Burning Bush
An unqualified man accepts an impossible mission.

### 4. David Faces Goliath
A sling, a stone, and total trust in God.

### 5. Daniel in the Lions' Den
Praying when prayer is punishable by death.

### 6. Shadrach, Meshach, Abednego
Even if God does not save us, we will not bow.

### 7. The Woman Who Touched Jesus' Cloak
If I can just touch His garment, I will be healed.

### 8. Peter Walks on Water
Eyes on Jesus, not on the waves.

### 9. The Centurion's Faith
Jesus says: I have not found faith like this in all of Israel.

### 10. Abraham and Isaac
Trusting God with the thing you love most.

### 11. Joshua Enters the Promised Land
Be strong and courageous.

### 12. Mary Says Yes
I am the Lord's servant.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best Bible story about faith for kids?

Noah Building the Ark is the most popular Bible story about faith for children because it has a clear, memorable example that kids can understand and apply to their own lives.

### How do I teach my child about faith using the Bible?

Start with one story from this list. Watch the video together (60 seconds on Faithful Kids), then ask your child what they noticed about faith in the story. Connect it to something in their own life. Repeat with a new story each week.

### What age are these stories appropriate for?

These stories are designed for children ages 5-12. Younger children (5-7) will understand the basic lesson. Older children (8-12) can discuss the deeper implications and apply the lessons more independently.
