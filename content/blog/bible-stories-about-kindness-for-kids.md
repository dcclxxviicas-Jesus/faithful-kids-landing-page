# 10 Bible Stories About Kindness for Kids (Be Kind Like Jesus)

Looking for Bible stories that teach your child about kindness? These 10 stories are the best examples of kindness in the Bible, retold simply for kids ages 5+.

## Why Teach Kids About Kindness Through Bible Stories?

Children learn values through stories, not lectures. When your child watches David face Goliath or Ruth stay loyal to Naomi, they are not just hearing about kindness. They are seeing it in action. These stories give kids a vocabulary for kindness that they carry into their own lives.

---

### 1. The Good Samaritan
Helping a stranger when nobody else would.

### 2. Ruth and Naomi
Loyalty and love across generations.

### 3. Jesus Heals the Sick
Touching people nobody else would touch.

### 4. Feeding the Five Thousand
A boy shares his lunch and Jesus multiplies it.

### 5. David and Mephibosheth
A king shows kindness to his enemy's grandson.

### 6. Boaz and Ruth
A man goes beyond what is required to help a widow.

### 7. Jesus and the Children
When disciples push kids away, Jesus welcomes them.

### 8. The Widow's Oil
Elisha helps a desperate mother.

### 9. Barnabas the Encourager
A man whose name literally means 'son of encouragement'.

### 10. Jesus Washes the Disciples' Feet
The King serves His servants.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best Bible story about kindness for kids?

The Good Samaritan is the most popular Bible story about kindness for children because it has a clear, memorable example that kids can understand and apply to their own lives.

### How do I teach my child about kindness using the Bible?

Start with one story from this list. Watch the video together (60 seconds on Faithful Kids), then ask your child what they noticed about kindness in the story. Connect it to something in their own life. Repeat with a new story each week.

### What age are these stories appropriate for?

These stories are designed for children ages 5-12. Younger children (5-7) will understand the basic lesson. Older children (8-12) can discuss the deeper implications and apply the lessons more independently.
