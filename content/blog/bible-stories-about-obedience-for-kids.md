# 10 Bible Stories About Obedience for Kids

Looking for Bible stories that teach your child about obedience? These 10 stories are the best examples of obedience in the Bible, retold simply for kids ages 5+.

## Why Teach Kids About Obedience Through Bible Stories?

Children learn values through stories, not lectures. When your child watches David face Goliath or Ruth stay loyal to Naomi, they are not just hearing about obedience. They are seeing it in action. These stories give kids a vocabulary for obedience that they carry into their own lives.

---

### 1. Noah Builds the Ark
God said build. Noah built. No questions..

### 2. Abraham Leaves Home
God said go. Abraham went..

### 3. Moses Goes to Pharaoh
God said speak. Moses spoke..

### 4. Joshua at Jericho
God said march. Joshua marched..

### 5. Jonah
God said go to Nineveh. Jonah ran the other way. It did not end well..

### 6. Daniel
The law said stop praying. Daniel kept praying..

### 7. Mary
The angel said you will have a son. Mary said: let it be..

### 8. The Rich Young Ruler
Jesus said give everything away. He could not do it..

### 9. Peter's Net
Jesus said cast your net. Peter had been fishing all night with nothing. He obeyed anyway..

### 10. The Ten Commandments
God gave rules. The people promised to follow them. Then came the golden calf..

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best Bible story about obedience for kids?

Noah Building the Ark is the most popular Bible story about obedience for children because it has a clear, memorable example that kids can understand and apply to their own lives.

### How do I teach my child about obedience using the Bible?

Start with one story from this list. Watch the video together (60 seconds on Faithful Kids), then ask your child what they noticed about obedience in the story. Connect it to something in their own life. Repeat with a new story each week.

### What age are these stories appropriate for?

These stories are designed for children ages 5-12. Younger children (5-7) will understand the basic lesson. Older children (8-12) can discuss the deeper implications and apply the lessons more independently.
