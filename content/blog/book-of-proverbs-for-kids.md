# The Book of Proverbs for Kids: Wisdom Made Simple

The Book of Proverbs is the Bible's instruction manual for living well. Most of it was written by King Solomon, who God called the wisest man who ever lived. When God offered Solomon anything he wanted, Solomon did not ask for money, power, or fame. He asked for wisdom. God was so pleased that He gave Solomon all of it (1 Kings 3:5-14).

Proverbs is a collection of short, memorable sayings that teach people how to make good decisions, treat others well, and live in a way that honors God. Think of each proverb as a life hack straight from the Creator of life.

For kids, Proverbs is pure gold. The wisdom in this book applies to playground conflicts, homework struggles, friendships, honesty, hard work, and every other challenge kids face daily. Here are 15 Proverbs every child should know, explained in language they can understand.

## 15 Proverbs Every Kid Should Know

### 1. Proverbs 3:5-6 — Trust God, Not Just Yourself

**"Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding; in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight."**

What it means for kids: You do not have to figure everything out on your own. When you are confused about what to do, ask God. He can see the whole road, even when you can only see one step ahead. Trusting God means believing His plan is better than yours.

Real-life example: When your best friend moves away and you do not understand why, trust that God has something good planned even though you cannot see it yet.

### 2. Proverbs 22:6 — Start Young

**"Start children off on the way they should go, and even when they are old they will not turn from it."**

What it means for kids: The habits you build now, like praying, being kind, and telling the truth, will stick with you when you grow up. What you practice as a kid, you become as an adult.

Real-life example: A child who practices saying "I'm sorry" when they are seven grows into an adult who knows how to admit mistakes at thirty.

### 3. Proverbs 17:17 — What Real Friends Do

**"A friend loves at all times, and a brother is born for a time of adversity."**

What it means for kids: A true friend does not disappear when things get hard. Real friends stick around when you are sad, when you mess up, and when everyone else walks away. That is the kind of friend to be and the kind of friend to look for.

Real-life example: When your friend does not make the soccer team and everyone else ignores them, a true friend sits with them at lunch and says, "I know that was tough."

### 4. Proverbs 15:1 — Soft Words Win

**"A gentle answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger."**

What it means for kids: When someone is angry at you and you yell back, the fight gets bigger. But when you respond calmly and kindly, it is like throwing water on a fire. You have the power to make a situation better or worse, just by the tone of your voice.

Real-life example: Your sibling accuses you of taking their toy. Instead of shouting "I didn't take it!" try saying calmly, "I understand you're upset. Let's find it together."

### 5. Proverbs 12:22 — God Loves Truth

**"The Lord detests lying lips, but he delights in people who are trustworthy."**

What it means for kids: God really, truly hates lying, and He really, truly loves honesty. Being trustworthy means people can count on your words. When you tell the truth even when it is hard, God smiles.

Real-life example: You accidentally broke a lamp. It is tempting to blame the dog, but telling your parent the truth, even knowing there will be consequences, is what makes God proud of you.

### 6. Proverbs 16:3 — Give Your Plans to God

**"Commit to the Lord whatever you do, and he will establish your plans."**

What it means for kids: Before you start something new, whether it is a school project, a sport, or a friendship, talk to God about it first. Ask Him to guide you. When you include God in your plans, He helps them succeed in ways you did not expect.

Real-life example: Before a big test, instead of just worrying, pray: "God, I studied hard. Please help me do my best and trust You with the results."

### 7. Proverbs 27:17 — Friends Make You Better

**"As iron sharpens iron, so one person sharpens another."**

What it means for kids: Good friends make you a better person. Just like a dull knife gets sharper when rubbed against another piece of iron, you grow when you are around people who challenge you to be your best. Choose friends who make you stronger, kinder, and braver.

Real-life example: A friend who says, "Hey, that was not nice. You should apologize," is a better friend than one who says, "Don't worry about it."

### 8. Proverbs 16:18 — Pride Goes Before a Fall

**"Pride goes before destruction, a haughty spirit before a fall."**

What it means for kids: When you start thinking you are better than everyone else, you are about to trip. Pride blinds you to your own weaknesses. Staying humble means knowing that your gifts come from God and that everyone around you has value too.

Real-life example: The kid who brags about winning every race is the one most embarrassed when they finally lose. The kid who wins humbly earns everyone's respect.

### 9. Proverbs 18:21 — Words Are Powerful

**"The tongue has the power of life and death, and those who love it will eat its fruit."**

What it means for kids: Your words can build someone up or tear them down. A kind word can make someone's entire day. A cruel word can hurt someone for years. Choose your words carefully because they are more powerful than you think.

Real-life example: Telling a classmate "You are really good at drawing" could be the encouragement they needed to keep going. Saying "Your drawing is ugly" could make them stop drawing forever.

### 10. Proverbs 3:9 — Give Your Best to God

**"Honor the Lord with your wealth, with the firstfruits of all your crops."**

What it means for kids: When you get something good, whether it is allowance money, a talent, or even your time, give the first and best part to God. Do not give God the leftovers. This teaches generosity and reminds us that everything we have comes from Him.

Real-life example: If you earn five dollars, put some in the offering at church before you spend the rest. If you are good at singing, use that gift to praise God, not just to show off.

### 11. Proverbs 19:20 — Listen to Advice

**"Listen to advice and accept discipline, and at the end you will be counted among the wise."**

What it means for kids: Wise people are not born wise. They become wise by listening to people who know more than they do. When your parents, teachers, or coaches give you advice, listen carefully. It might not feel good in the moment, but it will make you wiser over time.

### 12. Proverbs 14:29 — Be Slow to Anger

**"Whoever is patient has great understanding, but one who is quick-tempered displays folly."**

What it means for kids: When you get mad fast and react without thinking, you usually make things worse. But when you take a deep breath, count to ten, and think before you respond, that is true strength. Patience is not weakness; it is self-control.

### 13. Proverbs 11:25 — Generosity Comes Back

**"A generous person will prosper; whoever refreshes others will be refreshed."**

What it means for kids: When you give to others, whether it is sharing your snack, helping a classmate, or giving your time, good things come back to you. God designed the world so that generosity creates more generosity. The more you give, the more you receive.

### 14. Proverbs 20:11 — Your Actions Reveal Your Character

**"Even small children are known by their actions, so is their conduct really pure and upright?"**

What it means for kids: People watch what you do, not just what you say. If you say you are kind but you are mean to your sibling, your actions tell the real story. Your character is built by the choices you make every day, especially when no one is watching.

### 15. Proverbs 4:23 — Guard Your Heart

**"Above all else, guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it."**

What it means for kids: Your heart is like the control center of your life. What you put into it (what you watch, listen to, think about, and spend time on) shapes who you become. Guarding your heart means being careful about what you let influence you.

## How to Teach Proverbs to Kids

**One proverb per week.** Do not overwhelm kids with the entire book. Pick one proverb, write it on a sticky note, put it on the bathroom mirror, and talk about it during the week. By the end of a year, your child will have internalized dozens of life-changing truths.

**Connect them to real situations.** The best time to share a proverb is when it applies to something happening right now. Had a fight with a sibling? Proverbs 15:1. Struggling with a friend? Proverbs 17:17. Tempted to lie? Proverbs 12:22.

**Let kids see you living them.** Kids learn more from watching you than from hearing you. When you respond gently to frustration, give generously, or admit a mistake, you are teaching Proverbs without saying a word.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How old should kids be to start learning Proverbs?
Kids as young as 5-6 can grasp simple proverbs like "A gentle answer turns away wrath" (Proverbs 15:1). By age 8-10, children can understand more abstract proverbs about character, wisdom, and planning. The key is to always pair the proverb with a concrete example they can relate to.

### Who wrote the Book of Proverbs?
Most of Proverbs was written by King Solomon (Proverbs 1:1), who God granted extraordinary wisdom. Chapters 30 and 31 were written by Agur and King Lemuel, respectively. The book was compiled over several centuries as a collection of Israel's wisdom literature.

### How is Proverbs different from other books of the Bible?
Proverbs is not a story or a letter. It is a collection of wise sayings, short and memorable, designed to be practical. While other books tell you what happened, Proverbs tells you how to live. Think of it as the Bible's advice column.

### Are Proverbs promises from God?
Proverbs are general principles, not absolute guarantees. For example, "Train up a child in the way he should go" (22:6) is wise parenting advice, but it does not mean that every child raised well will make perfect choices. Proverbs describe how life generally works under God's design, not rigid formulas.
