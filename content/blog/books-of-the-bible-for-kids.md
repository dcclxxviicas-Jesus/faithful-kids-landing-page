# All 66 Books of the Bible for Kids (Simple Summaries)

The Bible is not just one book. It is a library of 66 books, written by about 40 different authors over approximately 1,500 years. Despite all those different writers and time periods, the whole Bible tells one big story: God loves people, people wander away from God, and God makes a way to bring them back.

Learning the books of the Bible gives kids a roadmap for this incredible library. When they hear a pastor say "Turn to Philippians," they will know where to look. When a friend mentions Psalm 23, they will know it is in the middle of the Bible. And when they want to read about Jesus, they will know to open one of the four Gospels.

Here is every book of the Bible, summarized in one to two sentences your child can understand.

## The Old Testament (39 Books)

The Old Testament covers everything from the creation of the world to about 400 years before Jesus was born. It is divided into five sections.

### The Law (5 Books) — Also Called the Pentateuch or Torah

These five books were written by Moses and lay the foundation for everything that follows.

1. **Genesis** — God creates the world, humans sin, and God chooses Abraham's family to bless all nations. Key stories: creation, Noah's ark, Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, and Joseph.

2. **Exodus** — God rescues the Israelites from slavery in Egypt through Moses and gives them the Ten Commandments. Key stories: baby Moses, the ten plagues, crossing the Red Sea.

3. **Leviticus** — God gives His people rules for worship, sacrifices, and holy living. It is like a handbook for how Israel should approach God.

4. **Numbers** — The Israelites wander in the wilderness for 40 years because they did not trust God. It includes a census (counting) of the people, which is how the book got its name.

5. **Deuteronomy** — Moses gives his final speeches to Israel before they enter the Promised Land. He reminds them of everything God has done and begs them to stay faithful.

### History (12 Books)

These books tell the story of Israel entering the Promised Land, establishing a kingdom, falling into exile, and returning home.

6. **Joshua** — Joshua leads the Israelites into the Promised Land and conquers it with God's help. Key stories: crossing the Jordan, the battle of Jericho.

7. **Judges** — After Joshua dies, Israel cycles through sin, suffering, and rescue by leaders called judges. Key figures: Deborah, Gideon, Samson.

8. **Ruth** — A loyal young woman named Ruth follows her mother-in-law Naomi to Israel and becomes the great-grandmother of King David. A beautiful story of faithfulness and love.

9. **1 Samuel** — Israel asks for a king and gets Saul, who starts well but falls away. God chooses a shepherd boy named David to replace him.

10. **2 Samuel** — David becomes king, unites Israel, and leads with faith, but also makes serious mistakes. A story of greatness, failure, and repentance.

11. **1 Kings** — Solomon builds the Temple and becomes famous for his wisdom, but the kingdom splits in two after his death. The northern kingdom is called Israel, the southern is called Judah.

12. **2 Kings** — Both kingdoms decline as most of their kings reject God. Israel falls to Assyria and Judah falls to Babylon. The Temple is destroyed.

13. **1 Chronicles** — A retelling of Israel's history from Adam to David, focusing on worship and the Temple. Like a highlights reel of the good parts.

14. **2 Chronicles** — A retelling of the kings of Judah, from Solomon to the exile in Babylon. Emphasizes the importance of faithful worship.

15. **Ezra** — The Israelites return from exile in Babylon and rebuild the Temple. Ezra leads a spiritual revival, calling people back to God's Word.

16. **Nehemiah** — Nehemiah leads the rebuilding of Jerusalem's walls despite opposition. A story about perseverance, prayer, and strong leadership.

17. **Esther** — A Jewish girl becomes queen of Persia and saves her people from a plot to destroy them. God's name is never mentioned, but His hand is everywhere.

### Poetry and Wisdom (5 Books)

These books explore life's biggest questions through songs, prayers, and wise sayings.

18. **Job** — A good man named Job suffers terribly and wrestles with the question of why. God answers, not with explanations, but with His presence. A book about trusting God in the dark.

19. **Psalms** — 150 songs and prayers covering every human emotion, from praise and joy to fear and anger. Written mostly by David. The Bible's songbook.

20. **Proverbs** — Short, wise sayings about how to live well, written mostly by King Solomon. Practical advice about honesty, friendship, hard work, and more.

21. **Ecclesiastes** — Solomon reflects on life and discovers that without God, everything is meaningless. But with God, life has purpose and joy.

22. **Song of Solomon** — A love poem between a husband and wife. It celebrates the beauty of love as God designed it.

### Major Prophets (5 Books)

These are called "major" because they are longer, not because they are more important.

23. **Isaiah** — Isaiah warns Israel about coming judgment but also gives beautiful prophecies about a Savior who is coming. Many of these point directly to Jesus.

24. **Jeremiah** — Known as the "weeping prophet," Jeremiah warns Judah for 40 years that judgment is coming, but they refuse to listen. He also promises that God will make a new covenant.

25. **Lamentations** — A collection of sad poems mourning the destruction of Jerusalem. Even in grief, the author trusts that God's mercies are "new every morning" (3:22-23).

26. **Ezekiel** — A prophet living in exile who has dramatic visions of God's glory. He promises that one day, God will give His people new hearts.

27. **Daniel** — Daniel and his friends stay faithful to God while living in Babylon. Key stories: the fiery furnace, the lion's den, and prophecies about future kingdoms.

### Minor Prophets (12 Books)

These are called "minor" because they are shorter, but their messages are powerful.

28. **Hosea** — God tells Hosea to marry an unfaithful wife as a picture of how Israel has been unfaithful to God. A story of heartbreak and relentless love.

29. **Joel** — A prophet who warns about God's judgment (the "Day of the Lord") but also promises that God will pour out His Spirit on all people.

30. **Amos** — A shepherd who boldly calls out Israel for oppressing the poor and ignoring justice. God cares about how we treat the vulnerable.

31. **Obadiah** — The shortest book in the Old Testament. A prophecy against Edom for celebrating when Jerusalem fell.

32. **Jonah** — God sends Jonah to preach to Israel's enemy, Nineveh. Jonah runs away, gets swallowed by a great fish, and finally obeys. A story about God's mercy for everyone.

33. **Micah** — A prophet who tells Israel what God truly wants: "To act justly, love mercy, and walk humbly with your God" (6:8).

34. **Nahum** — A prophecy about the destruction of Nineveh (the city Jonah preached to). God is patient, but He is also just.

35. **Habakkuk** — A prophet who questions God about why evil seems to win. God's answer: "The righteous will live by faith" (2:4).

36. **Zephaniah** — Warns about coming judgment but ends with a beautiful promise: God will rejoice over His people "with singing" (3:17).

37. **Haggai** — A prophet who encourages the returned exiles to finish rebuilding the Temple. "Is it time for you to live in your paneled houses while my house remains a ruin?" (1:4).

38. **Zechariah** — Full of visions about Israel's future and prophecies about the coming Messiah, including His entry into Jerusalem on a donkey (9:9).

39. **Malachi** — The last Old Testament prophet, who calls Israel to stop giving God their leftovers and start giving Him their best. Then 400 years of silence before Jesus arrives.

## The New Testament (27 Books)

The New Testament begins with the birth of Jesus and covers His life, death, resurrection, and the birth and growth of the early church.

### The Gospels (4 Books)

Four accounts of Jesus' life, each from a different perspective.

40. **Matthew** — Written for a Jewish audience, showing that Jesus is the promised Messiah and King. Includes the Sermon on the Mount and many of Jesus' parables.

41. **Mark** — The shortest Gospel, fast-paced and action-packed. Shows Jesus as a servant who came to give His life for others.

42. **Luke** — Written by a doctor, this is the most detailed Gospel. Emphasizes Jesus' compassion for outsiders, women, the poor, and sinners.

43. **John** — Focuses on who Jesus is (the Son of God) and why He came (so that everyone who believes in Him can have eternal life). Includes seven "I am" statements.

### History (1 Book)

44. **Acts** — The sequel to Luke's Gospel. Tells the story of the early church after Jesus returns to heaven, the coming of the Holy Spirit, and how the gospel spread from Jerusalem to Rome. Key figures: Peter and Paul.

### Paul's Letters (13 Books)

Letters written by the apostle Paul to churches and individuals.

45. **Romans** — Paul's masterpiece explaining the gospel: everyone has sinned, Jesus saves us by grace through faith, and now we live in the power of the Holy Spirit.

46. **1 Corinthians** — Paul addresses problems in the Corinthian church: division, immorality, and confusion about spiritual gifts. Includes the famous "love chapter" (chapter 13).

47. **2 Corinthians** — Paul defends his ministry and teaches that God's power is made perfect in our weakness.

48. **Galatians** — A passionate letter arguing that salvation comes through faith in Jesus, not by following rules. "It is for freedom that Christ has set us free" (5:1).

49. **Ephesians** — Teaches about the incredible riches of God's grace and how the church is one body united in Christ. Includes the "armor of God" passage (6:10-18).

50. **Philippians** — A joyful letter written from prison. Paul's key message: rejoice in all circumstances. "I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me" (4:13).

51. **Colossians** — Jesus is supreme over all creation. Everything was made by Him and for Him.

52. **1 Thessalonians** — Encourages believers to live holy lives and gives hope about Jesus' return.

53. **2 Thessalonians** — Corrects misunderstandings about Jesus' return and encourages hard work while waiting.

54. **1 Timothy** — Paul advises his young protege Timothy on leading a church well.

55. **2 Timothy** — Paul's final letter, written before his death. "I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith" (4:7).

56. **Titus** — Instructions for Titus on organizing churches on the island of Crete.

57. **Philemon** — A short personal letter asking Philemon to forgive and welcome back his runaway slave, Onesimus, now a fellow believer.

### General Letters (8 Books)

58. **Hebrews** — Shows that Jesus is greater than everything in the Old Testament: greater than angels, Moses, the priesthood, and the sacrificial system.

59. **James** — Practical wisdom about living out your faith. "Faith without deeds is dead" (2:26).

60. **1 Peter** — Encouragement for Christians facing suffering. Stay faithful because your reward is coming.

61. **2 Peter** — Warns against false teachers and reminds believers that God is patient, not slow.

62. **1 John** — "God is love." Tests for knowing if your faith is real: do you love others, obey God, and believe in Jesus?

63. **2 John** — A short letter urging believers to walk in truth and love.

64. **3 John** — A personal letter praising hospitality and warning against a controlling church leader.

65. **Jude** — A warning against false teachers who twist God's grace into an excuse for sin.

### Prophecy (1 Book)

66. **Revelation** — The final book of the Bible, written by John. Full of dramatic visions about the end of the world, the defeat of evil, and the creation of a new heaven and new earth where God lives with His people forever.

## Memory Tips for Kids

**Group them by section.** Instead of memorizing all 66 at once, learn them five at a time by section. Start with the five Law books, then the History books, and so on.

**Sing the books.** There are dozens of "Books of the Bible" songs available online. Music makes memorization dramatically easier. Find one your child likes and play it in the car.

**Use hand motions.** Associate each section with a gesture. Law: hands like stone tablets. History: marching feet. Poetry: hands on heart. Prophets: hand shading eyes (looking ahead). Gospels: open book. Letters: writing motion.

**Make it a game.** Quiz each other at dinner. "What comes after Nehemiah?" or "Which section is Philippians in?" Small prizes (stickers, extra dessert) make it fun.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take a kid to memorize all 66 books?
With regular practice (5-10 minutes a day), most kids ages 7-12 can learn all 66 books in order within 2-3 months. Songs and repetition are the most effective methods. Start with the section names, then fill in the individual books.

### What is the difference between the Old Testament and the New Testament?
The Old Testament (39 books) covers creation through about 400 BC and focuses on God's relationship with the nation of Israel. The New Testament (27 books) covers the life of Jesus and the early church. Together they tell one continuous story of God's plan to rescue humanity.

### Which book of the Bible should kids read first?
Mark is a great starting point because it is short, action-packed, and tells the story of Jesus clearly. For younger kids, Genesis is wonderful because of its vivid stories. The Psalms are perfect for kids who are learning to pray.

### Do Catholic Bibles have more books?
Yes. Catholic Bibles include 7 additional books called the Deuterocanonical books (or Apocrypha): Tobit, Judith, 1 and 2 Maccabees, Wisdom, Sirach, and Baruch, plus additions to Esther and Daniel. Protestant Bibles have 66 books, and Catholic Bibles have 73.
