## What Is the Story of Crossing the Red Sea for Kids?

Hey, welcome back! Buckle up because today's story has one of the most jaw-dropping moments in the entire Bible. We're talking armies, impossible escapes, and an ocean splitting in half. Yeah. You heard me right.

So remember Pharaoh? The guy who finally — after TEN plagues — let my people go? Well, he changed his mind. Can you believe it? The frogs, the darkness, the firstborn — all of that — and this guy goes, "Wait... who's going to do all the work now?" So he gathers his entire army — chariots, horses, soldiers — and chases after Israel at full speed.

Picture this. The Israelites hear the thundering of hooves and chariot wheels behind them. They look back and see the most powerful army on earth charging at them. They look forward and see... the Red Sea. No boats. No bridges. No way out.

What would YOU do? Because the Israelites panicked. They yelled at Moses: "Did you bring us out here to DIE?" And I get it — fear is real. But here's what they forgot: God was right there with them.

This story comes from Exodus 13-15 and is part of the Old Testament.

## What Does the Story of Crossing the Red Sea Teach Children?

The story of Crossing the Red Sea teaches children important lessons about freedom, courage, God's power, obedience:

- **God fights for His people when they cannot fight for themselves**
- **Freedom sometimes requires walking through scary situations**
- **Trusting God means moving forward even when the path seems impossible**

## Key Bible Verse

> *"The Lord will fight for you; you need only to be still."* — Exodus 14:14

## Parent's Guide: Why This Story Matters

The crucifixion is the most important and most difficult story in the Bible to share with kids. Younger children (5-7) may need a gentler framing focused on Jesus's love and sacrifice. Older children (8+) can handle more of the weight. The key message for all ages: Jesus chose this because He loves us that much. This story is best paired with the Resurrection so your child sees the full picture: Friday was dark, but Sunday was coming.

## Quiz: Test What Your Child Learned

After watching the video, see if your child can answer these questions:

### What did Pharaoh do after he let the Israelites go?

Pharaoh regretted letting the Israelites leave and sent his entire army with chariots and horses to chase them down and bring them back.

### What did God use to part the Red Sea?

God told Moses to stretch his staff over the sea, and then God sent a powerful east wind that blew all night long, splitting the waters and creating dry ground in the middle.

### What did the Israelites do after they crossed the Red Sea safely?

After witnessing God's incredible rescue, the Israelites celebrated with singing and dancing. Miriam grabbed a tambourine, and they sang a song praising God as their strength and salvation.


## Discussion Questions for Families

Use these questions at the dinner table or during bedtime:

1. What was your favorite part of the story of Crossing the Red Sea?
2. What do you think God was teaching us through this story?
3. How can we be more like Crossing in our own lives?
4. If you were in this story, what would you have done differently?
5. What does this story teach us about trusting God?
