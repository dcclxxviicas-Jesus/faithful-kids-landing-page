The Philistine army lined up on one hill. Israel's army on the other. A valley between them. And out of the Philistine camp stepped a MONSTER — Goliath of Gath. Nine feet tall. Bronze helmet. Armor weighing 125 pounds. A spear with an iron tip that weighed 15 pounds alone. Walking. Tank.

Every morning and evening for FORTY DAYS, Goliath stomped into that valley and shouted: "Send me one man to fight! If he wins, we're your servants. If I win, you're OURS!" And every day, the entire army of Israel — including King Saul — shook with fear. Nobody moved. For forty days.

This story comes from 1 Samuel and is part of the Old Testament.

## What Does the Story of David and Goliath Teach Children?

The story of David and Goliath teaches children important lessons about courage, faith, God choosing the unlikely:

- **God does not look at size or strength but at the heart**
- **With God on your side, no problem is too big**
- **Sometimes the smallest person in the room has the biggest faith**

## Key Bible Verse

> *"The Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart."* — 1 Samuel 16:7

## Parent's Guide: Why This Story Matters

David and Goliath is the ultimate underdog story, and kids instinctively connect with it. Your child is small in a big world, and this story tells them that size does not determine who wins. God does. This is a powerful story to revisit when your child faces something that feels too big: a hard test, a bully, a new school. The lesson is not that they can do it alone, but that God is with them.

Use these questions at the dinner table or during bedtime:

5. What does this story teach us about trusting God?
