Friends, we've reached the end. This is the final episode of King David's story — and I have to tell you, it's one of the most beautiful moments in the entire Bible.

David is old. His battles are behind him. His songs have been written. His mistakes have been confessed. And now, one last time, he gathers all of Israel together for a farewell that will echo through eternity.

David stands before them and shares his greatest dream: the temple. All his life, David wanted to build a permanent house for God. But God had told him, "You won't build it, David. You've been a man of war. Your son Solomon will build my house."

## What Does the Story of Davids Final Words Teach Children?

The story of Davids Final Words teaches children important lessons about worship, repentance, God's promises, honesty:

- **God does not look at size or strength but at the heart**
- **With God on your side, no problem is too big**
- **Sometimes the smallest person in the room has the biggest faith**

## Key Bible Verse

> *"The Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart."* — 1 Samuel 16:7

## Parent's Guide: Why This Story Matters

David and Goliath is the ultimate underdog story, and kids instinctively connect with it. Your child is small in a big world, and this story tells them that size does not determine who wins. God does. This is a powerful story to revisit when your child faces something that feels too big: a hard test, a bully, a new school. The lesson is not that they can do it alone, but that God is with them.
