## What Does This Story Teach Kids?

The story of Feeding the Five Thousand teaches children important lessons about faith, God's power, compassion, trust. Here are the key takeaways for your child:

- **Trust in God** — Even when things seem impossible, God has a plan
- **Courage** — Standing up for what is right, even when it is hard
- **Faith** — Believing in God's promises even when you cannot see the outcome
- **Obedience** — Following God's instructions leads to blessings

## Quiz: Test What You Learned

After watching the video, see if your child can answer these questions:

### Which disciple did Jesus ask about buying bread for the crowd?

Jesus asked Philip where they could buy bread, testing him — because Jesus already knew what He was going to do.

### How much did Philip say it would cost to feed the crowd?

Philip calculated that more than eight months' wages wouldn't be enough to give everyone even a small bite.

### What food did the boy have?

Andrew brought a boy who had five small barley loaves and two small fish — a simple, small lunch.

### How many people were fed (men only, not counting women and children)?

The Bible says about five thousand men were fed, plus women and children — so the total crowd was likely much larger.

### How many baskets of leftovers were collected?

After everyone ate and was full, the disciples collected twelve baskets of leftover bread — more than they started with!


## Discussion Questions for Parents

Use these questions at the dinner table or during bedtime to talk about the story with your child:

1. What was your favorite part of the story of Feeding the Five Thousand?
2. What do you think God was teaching us through this story?
3. How can we be more like Feeding in our own lives?
4. If you were in this story, what would you have done?
5. What does this story teach us about trusting God?
