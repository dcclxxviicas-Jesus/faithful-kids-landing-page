## What Is the Story of Gods Promise to David for Kids?

KING DAVID'S REIGN — EPISODE 2: GOD'S PROMISE TO DAVID Based on 2 Samuel 7 | Narrated by Jesus

Hey, friends! Last time, David danced the Ark right into Jerusalem. Today, something even bigger happens — God makes a promise that echoes through all of history. A promise that leads directly to Me.

David is settled in his beautiful cedar palace. And one night, it bothers him. He says to Nathan the prophet, "I am living in this gorgeous palace, but the Ark of God is sitting in a tent! I want to build God a house — a temple worthy of His name."

Nathan says, "Go ahead, do whatever is on your heart. God is with you." Sounds like a great plan, right?

This story comes from 2 Samuel/Psalms and is part of the Old Testament.

## What Does the Story of Gods Promise to David Teach Children?

The story of Gods Promise to David teaches children important lessons about worship, repentance, God's promises, honesty:

- **God does not look at size or strength but at the heart**
- **With God on your side, no problem is too big**
- **Sometimes the smallest person in the room has the biggest faith**

## Key Bible Verse

> *"The Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart."* — 1 Samuel 16:7

## Parent's Guide: Why This Story Matters

David and Goliath is the ultimate underdog story, and kids instinctively connect with it. Your child is small in a big world, and this story tells them that size does not determine who wins. God does. This is a powerful story to revisit when your child faces something that feels too big: a hard test, a bully, a new school. The lesson is not that they can do it alone, but that God is with them.
