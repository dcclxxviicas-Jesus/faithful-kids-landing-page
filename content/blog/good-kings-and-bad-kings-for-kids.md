## What Does This Story Teach Kids?

The story of Good Kings and Bad Kings teaches children important lessons about wisdom, worship, consequences, choices. Here are the key takeaways for your child:

- **Trust in God** — Even when things seem impossible, God has a plan
- **Courage** — Standing up for what is right, even when it is hard
- **Faith** — Believing in God's promises even when you cannot see the outcome
- **Obedience** — Following God's instructions leads to blessings

## Quiz: Test What You Learned

After watching the video, see if your child can answer these questions:

### What did Jeroboam set up in the Northern Kingdom that led Israel into idol worship?

Jeroboam was afraid his people would return to Jerusalem to worship, so he built two golden calves — one in Dan and one in Bethel — and told the people these were their gods. It echoed the same sin Israel committed at Mount Sinai.

### What did King Hezekiah do when the Assyrian army surrounded Jerusalem?

Instead of panicking or surrendering, Hezekiah brought the threatening letter before God in the temple and prayed for deliverance. That night, God sent an angel who defeated the Assyrian army of 185,000 soldiers.

### How old was Josiah when he became king, and what did he find that changed the nation?

Josiah became king at just eight years old. When he was about twenty-six, workers repairing the temple discovered the forgotten Book of the Law. When Josiah heard it read, he wept, tore down every idol in the land, and led the greatest spiritual revival Judah had ever seen.

4. If you were in this story, what would you have done?
5. What does this story teach us about trusting God?
