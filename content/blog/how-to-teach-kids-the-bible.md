# How to Teach Your Kids the Bible (Even If You Are Not a Bible Expert)

You do not need a theology degree. You do not need to have read the Bible cover to cover. You just need to start with one story, one conversation, and one day at a time.

## Step 1: Start with the Stories, Not the Rules

Kids connect with stories, not theology. Do not start with "you should read the Bible because it is important." Start with "let me tell you about a boy who fought a giant" or "did you know a man once lived inside a fish?"

## Step 2: Pick One Story Per Week

Do not overwhelm your child. One story per week is plenty. Watch the 60-second video on Faithful Kids, take the quiz together, and talk about it over dinner.

## Step 3: Use Questions, Not Lectures

After the story, ask:
- "What was your favorite part?"
- "What do you think God was teaching us?"
- "When have you felt like that character?"

These questions do more teaching than any lecture ever will.

## Step 4: Make It Part of Your Routine

The best Bible teaching happens consistently, not intensely. A 60-second story at bedtime every night is better than a 30-minute Bible study once a month.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What if I do not know the Bible well enough to teach my kids?

That is exactly why tools like Faithful Kids exist. The video teaches the story. The quiz checks understanding. You do not need to be an expert. You just need to be there.

### What age should I start teaching my kids the Bible?

You can start as early as 2-3 with simple stories (Creation, Noah, Baby Jesus). Ages 5+ can handle the full range of Bible stories with comprehension quizzes.
