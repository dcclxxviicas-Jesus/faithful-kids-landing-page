## What Is the Story of John the Baptist?

Alright, buckle up — we're fast-forwarding about eighteen years. Last time you saw me, I was twelve. Now I've grown up, and something wild is happening in the wilderness.

This story comes from Matthew 3:1-12, Luke 3:1-18 and is part of the New Testament.

## What Does This Story Teach Kids?

The story of John the Baptist teaches children important lessons about hope, faith, God's plan, Christmas, miracles. Here are the key takeaways for your child:

- **Trust in God** — Even when things seem impossible, God has a plan
- **Courage** — Standing up for what is right, even when it is hard
- **Faith** — Believing in God's promises even when you cannot see the outcome
- **Obedience** — Following God's instructions leads to blessings

## Key Bible Verse

> *"Trust in the Lord with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding."* — Proverbs 3:5

This verse connects to the story of John the Baptist because it reminds us that God's plan is always bigger than what we can see.

## Quiz: Test What You Learned

After watching the video, see if your child can answer these questions:

### What was John the Baptist's main message to the people?

John's urgent message was "Repent!" — calling people to turn away from their sins and prepare their hearts because God's Kingdom was about to arrive through Jesus.

### What did John call the Pharisees and Sadducees when they came to see him?

John boldly called the religious leaders a "brood of vipers" — a bunch of snakes — because they looked religious on the outside but hadn't truly changed their hearts.

### What did John say about the one coming after him?

John made it clear that Jesus was infinitely greater than him. While John baptized with water, Jesus would baptize with the Holy Spirit and fire — something only God could do.


## Discussion Questions for Parents

Use these questions at the dinner table or during bedtime to talk about the story with your child:

1. What was your favorite part of the story of John the Baptist?
2. What do you think God was teaching us through this story?
3. How can we be more like John in our own lives?
4. If you were in this story, what would you have done?
5. What does this story teach us about trusting God?
