## Quiz: Test What You Learned

After watching the video, see if your child can answer these questions:

### What was Saul doing before he met Jesus on the road to Damascus?

Saul was the most dangerous enemy of the early church. He was breathing murderous threats, going house to house to drag believers to prison, and was on his way to Damascus with official letters to arrest more Christians.

### What happened to Saul when the bright light appeared on the road?

A light brighter than the sun knocked Saul to the ground, and he heard Jesus's voice. When he opened his eyes, he was completely blind. He stayed blind for three days — no food, no water — until God sent Ananias to heal him.

### Why was Ananias afraid when God told him to go to Saul?

Ananias had heard all about Saul's reputation — he knew Saul had come to Damascus specifically to arrest believers. But God told Ananias that Saul was His "chosen instrument," and Ananias obeyed despite his fear.


## Discussion Questions for Parents

Use these questions at the dinner table or during bedtime to talk about the story with your child:

1. What was your favorite part of the story of Saul Meets Jesus?
2. What do you think God was teaching us through this story?
3. How can we be more like Saul in our own lives?
4. If you were in this story, what would you have done?
5. What does this story teach us about trusting God?
