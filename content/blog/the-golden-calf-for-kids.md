## What Is the Story of The Golden Calf?

Okay, friends. Today's story is a rough one — the kind that makes you want to shout at the screen. But it's important, because it shows something true about people and something even MORE true about God's mercy.

This story comes from Exodus 32 and is part of the Old Testament.

## What Does This Story Teach Kids?

The story of The Golden Calf teaches children important lessons about freedom, courage, God's power, obedience. Here are the key takeaways for your child:

- **Trust in God** — Even when things seem impossible, God has a plan
- **Courage** — Standing up for what is right, even when it is hard
- **Faith** — Believing in God's promises even when you cannot see the outcome
- **Obedience** — Following God's instructions leads to blessings

## Quiz: Test What You Learned

After watching the video, see if your child can answer these questions:

### Why did the Israelites ask Aaron to make them a god?

Moses was on Mount Sinai for forty days receiving instructions from God, and the people grew impatient and scared that he wasn't coming back, so they demanded a visible god to follow.

### What did Moses do when he came down the mountain and saw the golden calf?

Moses was so angry at what the people had done that he threw down the stone tablets God had written on, shattering them. Then he burned the golden calf, ground it into powder, mixed it with water, and made the people drink it.

### What did Moses do for the people after their terrible sin with the golden calf?

Even though the people had done something awful, Moses loved them and interceded for them. He went back up the mountain and pleaded with God to forgive the people, even offering to be erased from God's book if that's what it took.
