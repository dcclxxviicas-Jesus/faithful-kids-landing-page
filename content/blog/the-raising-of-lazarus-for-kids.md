## What Does This Story Teach Kids?

The story of The Raising of Lazarus teaches children important lessons about faith, God's power, compassion, trust. Here are the key takeaways for your child:

- **Trust in God** — Even when things seem impossible, God has a plan
- **Courage** — Standing up for what is right, even when it is hard
- **Faith** — Believing in God's promises even when you cannot see the outcome
- **Obedience** — Following God's instructions leads to blessings

## Quiz: Test What You Learned

After watching the video, see if your child can answer these questions:

### Where did Lazarus live with his sisters Mary and Martha?

Lazarus and his sisters Mary and Martha lived in the village of Bethany, near Jerusalem. Jesus was close friends with this family.

### How long did Jesus wait before going to see Lazarus?

After hearing that Lazarus was sick, Jesus intentionally stayed where He was for two more days before traveling to Bethany.

### How many days had Lazarus been in the tomb when Jesus arrived?

Lazarus had been dead and in the tomb for four days by the time Jesus arrived — making the miracle even more undeniable.

### What is the shortest verse in the Bible, found in this story?

"Jesus wept" (John 11:35) is the shortest verse in the Bible. It shows that Jesus felt deep sorrow and compassion for His friends.

### What did Jesus shout at the tomb?

Jesus cried out in a loud voice, "Lazarus, come out!" — and Lazarus walked out of the tomb alive, still wrapped in grave clothes.
