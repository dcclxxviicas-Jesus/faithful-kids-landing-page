## What Is the Story of The Wise Men?

Hey, welcome back! Last time we talked about the shepherds who came running to see me on the night I was born. But they weren't the only ones looking for me. Far, far away — in the East — something incredible was happening.

This story comes from Matthew 2:1-12 and is part of the New Testament.

## What Does This Story Teach Kids?

The story of The Wise Men teaches children important lessons about hope, faith, God's plan, Christmas, miracles. Here are the key takeaways for your child:

- **Trust in God** — Even when things seem impossible, God has a plan
- **Courage** — Standing up for what is right, even when it is hard
- **Faith** — Believing in God's promises even when you cannot see the outcome
- **Obedience** — Following God's instructions leads to blessings

## Quiz: Test What You Learned

After watching the video, see if your child can answer these questions:

### How did the wise men know a new King had been born?

The wise men were studying the heavens when a brand-new, extraordinary star appeared — and they recognized it as a sign that the King of the Jews had been born.

### Why did King Herod ask the wise men to report back to him about the child?

Herod pretended he wanted to worship Jesus, but he was actually threatened by the idea of another King and secretly planned to kill Him.

### What did the three gifts of the wise men represent?

Each gift pointed to who Jesus truly was — gold honored Him as King, frankincense recognized Him as God, and myrrh foreshadowed His future sacrifice for the world.


## Discussion Questions for Parents

Use these questions at the dinner table or during bedtime to talk about the story with your child:

1. What was your favorite part of the story of The Wise Men?
2. What do you think God was teaching us through this story?
3. How can we be more like The in our own lives?
4. If you were in this story, what would you have done?
5. What does this story teach us about trusting God?
